Susan B. Anthony

By Me

Susan B. Anthony was a prominent women's rights advocate and leader of the women's suffrage movement. Her work over five decades helped pave the way for the 1920 ratification of the 19th Amendment, granting women the right to vote. Anthony was raised in a Quaker family with a history of activism and a belief in equality under God. She became involved in the anti-slavery movement and temperance movement, and was known for her passionate anti-slavery speeches.
